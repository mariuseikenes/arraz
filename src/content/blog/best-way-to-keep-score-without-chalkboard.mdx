# Best Way to Keep Score in Darts Without a Chalkboard
### 2026-03-29

A chalkboard is the classic way to keep score in darts, but it is far from the only option. Many players now use phones, tablets, paper sheets, or digital scoreboards instead. For casual games at home, pub nights, or regular practice sessions, these options are often faster, easier to read, and less error-prone.


If you want the short answer, the best way to keep score in darts without a chalkboard is to use a dedicated darts scoring app. It reduces mental math, keeps scores visible, and makes games easier to manage, especially for formats like 301, 501, and Cricket.

## Why replace a chalkboard?

While chalkboards do have a certain charm to them, there are a few downsides:

- They require someone to write after every turn
- Scores can be hard to read from a distance, or with poor handwriting (I suffer from this)
- Mental math sometimes leads to mistakes, especially if there's a few beers involved
- Not portable

They're also an extra piece of equipment you need to purchase if you don't already own one,
in addition to taking up space. If you play in different places or want something easier to use on a phone, a digital option makes more sense.

## The most common alternatives

There are a few practical ways to keep score without using a chalkboard.

### 1. Darts scoring apps

A digital darts scorer is usually the best all-round option.

Why it works well:

- Scores update instantly
- The app handles subtraction and game logic
- It is easy to use for 301, 501, and other common formats
- Players can focus more on the match and less on the arithmetic

This is especially useful if you play often, rotate players, or want a cleaner experience than writing scores by hand.

### 2. Paper score sheets

Paper is simple and reliable.

Pros:

- No battery needed
- Easy to set up
- Works anywhere
- You don't rely on the game you're playing being available on the site.

Cons:

- Still requires manual math
- Easy to lose or damage
- Harder to correct mistakes cleanly
- Who walks around with a pen and paper everywhere these days?

Paper works well, but it is not the most convenient solution.

### 4. Spreadsheet or notes app

Some players use a spreadsheet, phone notes app, or basic calculator.
A spreadsheet can be nice for the number-nerds out there as it allows for more complex stat calculations,
but it's a hassle to set up and creates friction.

## So what is actually the best option?

For most players, a darts scoring app is the best option because it combines convenience, visibility, and accuracy.

It is better than a chalkboard when you want to:

- avoid mental math mistakes
- keep games moving
- use your phone or tablet instead of wall space
- support multiple game formats
- keep score during practice or casual matches

Overall, more practical. Though I'll admit a chalkboard in a pub does have a lot more charm to it.
